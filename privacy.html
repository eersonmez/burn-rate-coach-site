Privacy policy

Burn Rate Coach is an independent Chrome extension that adds pacing guidance to the Codex Analytics usage page. It is not affiliated with or endorsed by OpenAI.

Information handled

Burn Rate Coach processes quota information already visible on the Codex Analytics usage page: remaining percentages, meter names, reset times, and values derived from those fields. It also stores the settings you choose, including your target remaining percentage, tolerance, and history preference.

When local history is enabled, the extension stores timestamped quota percentages and reset timestamps in Chrome’s local extension storage. Each time the Analytics page processes history, records older than 10 days are ignored and pruned and each meter is capped at 2,600 samples. If Analytics is not opened again, older stored records can remain on the device until you clear history or uninstall the extension.

Burn Rate Coach does not read or store prompts, conversations, source code, passwords, authentication cookies, payment information, or API keys.

How information is used

The information is used only to calculate and display current pacing position, recommended action, local trajectories, projected remaining quota at reset, and the extension’s latest compatibility state.

Storage and transmission

Settings, compatibility state, and quota history remain in chrome.storage.local on your device. Burn Rate Coach has no backend, does not transmit quota information to the developer or third parties, and uses no analytics, advertising SDKs, or remote code.

When you follow a privacy or support link, Chrome opens a public website. Burn Rate Coach adds no tracking parameters and receives no data about the visit.

Sharing

Burn Rate Coach does not share, sell, or transfer extension data. It does not use data for advertising, profiling, creditworthiness, or any purpose unrelated to quota pacing.

Your controls

Turning off Local pace history stops collecting and using saved samples. Existing samples remain until the next Analytics cleanup, an explicit clear, or uninstall, and can inform projections if history is re-enabled first. The two-click Clear history control deletes current and legacy snapshots. If history remains enabled, later observations can begin a new local history.

Changes

Material changes to data handling will be reflected in this policy, the Chrome Web Store privacy disclosures, and—when required—an in-product disclosure before the changed handling begins.
